Sri Lankan Flavors: A Journey Through Authentic Tastes

Welcome to a delightful collection of Sri Lanka’s finest culinary creations, blending tradition and modern flavors. From wholesome vegetarian dishes to rich meat and seafood curries, from simple fruit juices to indulgent sweets, this section covers it all. Whether it’s a comforting rice and curry meal, crispy hoppers, flavorful kottu, or traditional desserts like watalappan, you’ll find something to satisfy your cravings. Explore the essence of Sri Lankan cuisine and discover the joy of authentic, home-cooked flavors.

    Gotukola Sambol (Pennywort Salad)

    Green leafy vegetables aren’t the most common thing to eat in Sri Lanka, though the cooking does call for many tuberous vegetables. But anyway, since I love green veggies, I ate a dish known as Gotukola Sambol, frequently during my visit. Gotukola is the word for Asiatic pennywort, a small leafy green vegetable that’s common throughout southeast Asia. Sambol is the word used to describe a dish or garnish that prepared and eaten using raw ingredients. So gotukola sambol is a basically Sri Lankan salad garnish.

  4. Traditional Dishes

    5. RECIPE

    Pittu

    Sri Lankan Pittu – A beloved traditional dish made from a mixture of rice flour and freshly grated coconut, steamed to perfection in a cylindrical mold. This soft, crumbly delicacy is enjoyed with a variety of accompaniments, such as coconut milk, spicy curries, or lunu miris for an extra kick. Whether paired with a hearty meat curry or a simple dhal curry, Pittu remains a staple in Sri Lankan cuisine, loved for its unique texture and deliciously comforting taste.

    RECIPE

    Roti

    Sri Lankan Roti – A traditional flatbread made with a blend of flour, coconut, and a pinch of salt, kneaded into a soft dough and cooked on a griddle. Variations include Pol Roti (coconut roti), Wheat Roti, and stuffed versions filled with spicy onions, cheese, or meats. Roti is best enjoyed warm with lunu miris, dhal curry, or a rich meat curry. This hearty dish is a staple for breakfast or dinner and pairs beautifully with a hot cup of Ceylon tea.

    RECIPE

    Malu Abul Thiyal

    Sri Lankan Malu Abul Thiyal – A unique and flavorful Sri Lankan fish curry that is known for its rich, tangy taste and ability to stay fresh for days without refrigeration. Made with firm fish like tuna, this dish is prepared by marinating the fish in a blend of spices, including black pepper, garlic, and most importantly, goraka (a souring agent). The fish is then slow-cooked in a clay pot until it absorbs the deep, smoky flavors of the spices. Traditionally enjoyed with rice, Malu Abul Thiyal is a must-try for those who love bold and authentic Sri Lankan flavors.

    RECIPE

    Kiribath (Milk Rice)

    Kiribath – A sacred and symbolic dish in Sri Lankan culture, Kiribath (Milk Rice) is made by cooking rice in coconut milk until it reaches a rich, creamy texture. More than just a meal, Kiribath is an essential dish served during New Year celebrations, weddings, birthdays, housewarmings, and other auspicious occasions .It represents prosperity, new beginnings, and is often shared among family and friends as a gesture of goodwill and blessings for the future. In Sri Lankan culture, the act of offering Kiribath symbolizes harmony and good fortune.

    RECIPE

    Hoppers

    Hoppers – A crispy, bowl-shaped Sri Lankan delicacy made from a fermented rice flour and coconut milk batter. With its delightfully crisp edges and soft, spongy center, hoppers are a versatile dish enjoyed for breakfast, dinner, or even as a snack. They pair perfectly with spicy sambols, rich curries, or even a simple egg, creating a perfect balance of flavor and texture. Whether plain or with variations like egg hoppers, this dish remains a beloved staple in Sri Lankan cuisine.

    RECIPE

    String Hoppers (Idiyappam)

    String Hoppers (Idiyappam) is a traditional Sri Lankan dish made from rice flour. It is typically served for breakfast or dinner, accompanied by curry or sambol. The dough is made by mixing rice flour with hot water and a pinch of salt, then pressing it into fine strings using a special tool called an "Idiyappam maker." The dough is steamed to create soft, delicate strands of rice flour. String hoppers are usually served with coconut milk, dhal curry, or a variety of vegetable or meat curries.

  8. Herbal Drinks

    9. RECIPE

    Kolakenda (Herbal Green Porridge)

    Kolakenda – A traditional herbal drink that has been a staple in Sri Lankan households for generations, Kolakenda is a nourishing and rejuvenating green porridge made from a blend of leafy herbs, rice, and coconut milk. Often infused with ingredients like Gotukola (pennywort), murunga (drumstick leaves), and other local herbs, this wholesome drink is packed with essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. Kolakenda is known for its detoxifying properties, promoting digestion, boosting immunity, and providing a natural energy lift. Whether enjoyed as a breakfast or an afternoon refresher, Kolakenda offers a natural, healthy alternative to sugary drinks, bringing the essence of Sri Lankan herbal wellness to your cup.

    RECIPE

    Koththamalli (Coriander Drink)

    Koththamalli – A refreshing herbal drink made from fresh coriander leaves, Koththamalli is not only a flavorful beverage but also a powerhouse of health benefits. Known for its detoxifying and anti-inflammatory properties, this drink is often consumed to improve digestion, reduce bloating, and promote overall wellness. Coriander, with its fresh, vibrant aroma and earthy taste, is blended into a smooth drink, sometimes with the addition of lime juice and honey for a zesty twist. Whether consumed after meals for its digestive benefits or simply as a revitalizing pick-me-up, Koththamalli is an invigorating addition to a healthy lifestyle, offering a natural way to boost energy and refresh the body.

    RECIPE

    Ranawara (Cassia Auriculata Drink)

    Ranawara – A soothing herbal tea made from the dried flowers of the Cassia Auriculata plant, Ranawara is widely known for its cooling and medicinal properties. This caffeine-free beverage helps to regulate body heat, purify blood, and promote relaxation. It is also believed to support reproductive health, improve skin complexion, and aid in digestion. With a mild, slightly sweet flavor, Ranawara is often enjoyed warm, sometimes with honey for added taste. A natural and refreshing herbal infusion, it is a great addition to a healthy lifestyle.
